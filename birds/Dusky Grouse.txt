Finally, I have found my birding nemesis.  After nearly two decades of attempts at this bird, I finally found some.  On my way up the canyon, I would stop in areas that looked promising and would walk with my dog up the road to see if we could find the grouse.  On the third stop, we were walking when just a few meters up the road, burst out a Dusky Grouse that flew upslope and landed.  It was obviously concerned with us and continued to watch us as it called.  I believe it probably had young stashed in the riparian thicket that it was communicating with.  I was able to get some photos of the bird.  This bird was seen at 0950 at 39.39.38N, -114.40.17W at 7510ft elevation.  Then as we continued to walk uphill, around the next bend there was a hen walking in the road with her tail fanned and upright.  She quickly flew up into a tree above the road and called.  I got some nice photos before she flew down stream.  I continued up the road another 100 meters or so and to my right a bunch of grouse burst into the air scattering in three different directions.  Two clearly young birds flew to the streamside, two more young birds flew downhill and the hen flew up into a tree and began calling continuously as if to encourage her scattered young to remain in cover until the threat, my dog and me, moved on.  I got some nice photos of her calling in the tree before continuing on.  We hiked for another 30 minutes upstream but did not encounter any more grouse.  On the way back down we flushed the mother and two young again in the same area.  This time the hen and one young bird about 1/3 her size perched in the same tree for photos.  What a great experience.  Photos will be added when I get them off my camera and processed.
